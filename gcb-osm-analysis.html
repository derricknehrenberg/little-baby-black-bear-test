    Nomination criteria check (BOLT / EXPLORE Act)

    • PASS≥ 80 miles: route network totals 117 mi.
    • PASSPrimarily existing dirt or natural-surface roads & trails: 82% follows existing mapped infrastructure; ~98% of the matched mileage is natural-surface or unpaved (only 1.9 mi matched to paved ways).
    • FLAGOpen to bicycle use: 2.2 mi rides Van Tuyl Trail 431, tagged bicycle=no in OSM. Zoom to it — confirm local rules or reroute.
    • VERIFYPrimarily federally managed lands (BLM / USFS / NPS / FWS / BoR): not analyzed here. The corridor is largely Gunnison NF / BLM, but valley-bottom segments near Gunnison and Crested Butte likely cross city, county, and private land — check against PAD-US or county parcels.
    • VERIFYPublicly accessible: OSM match does not prove legal access; verify the unmatched 20.6 mi and any private-land crossings.
    • NOTEPreference criteria: agencies favor geographic balance and proposals with local partners or volunteer organizations for maintenance — worth naming partners in the submission. Deadline Aug 3, 2026 (18 days from this analysis).

    Method. The KML (4 sections, 235 line strings) was densified to 10 m intervals; each interval was matched to the nearest OpenStreetMap way (all highway=* ways in the route bounding box, 3,082 ways, Overpass snapshot 2026-05-31). Intervals within 25 m of a mapped way are “existing”, classified by tag: trail = path / footway / cycleway / bridleway / steps; 4WD track = track; road = all other road classes. Results are threshold-stable: matched share moves only 85.8% → 86.4% as tolerance varies 15 m → 40 m.

    Caveats. “No OSM match” is not proof of new construction — unmapped social or user-built tread shows up the same way. OSM tags are incomplete (about half the local track/path ways carry no surface tag), an OSM match does not establish legal bicycle access or public access, land ownership was not analyzed, and mileages are map-line miles. Verify flagged items against USFS/BLM travel-management data before submitting.
