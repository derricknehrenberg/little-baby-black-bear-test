Comparison of LittleBabyBlackBear.kml (revised draft from Tim Kugler, Gunnison Trails) against OpenStreetMap. The drawn route is classified into segments on existing mapped trails, 4WD tracks, and roads versus segments with no mapped counterpart, in support of the DOI/USDA long-distance bike route self-nomination (EXPLORE Act — Bicycling on Long-Distance Trails). Consecutive OSM ways sharing a name are joined, so each named trail or road reads as one segment on the map and in the list below.

      Nomination criteria check (BOLT / EXPLORE Act)

      • PASS≥ 80 miles: route network totals 126 mi.
      • PASSPrimarily existing dirt or natural-surface roads & trails: 78% follows existing mapped infrastructure; ~97% of the matched mileage is natural-surface or unpaved (only 2.7 mi matched to paved ways).
      • PASSOpen to bicycle use: no matched ways restrict bicycles. The OSM way named “Van Tuyl Trail 431” (tagged bicycle=no) is, per Gunnison Trails, a mis-named segment of Three Mile Creek Trail 434 that allows bikes — treated as such here. Worth correcting the name and tag upstream in OSM.
      • VERIFYPrimarily federally managed lands (BLM / USFS / NPS / FWS / BoR): not analyzed here. The corridor is largely Gunnison NF / BLM, but valley-bottom segments near Gunnison and Crested Butte likely cross city, county, and private land — check against PAD-US or county parcels.
      • VERIFYPublicly accessible: OSM match does not prove legal access; verify the unmatched 27.9 mi and any private-land crossings.
      • NOTEPreference criteria: agencies favor geographic balance and proposals with local partners or volunteer organizations for maintenance — Gunnison Trails’ involvement is worth stating explicitly in the submission. Deadline Aug 3, 2026 (14 days from this analysis).

      Method. The KML (4 sections; fragmented export stitched into continuous chains) was densified to 10 m intervals; each interval was matched to the nearest OpenStreetMap way (all highway=* ways in the route bounding box, 3,082 ways, Overpass snapshot 2026-05-31). Intervals within 25 m of a mapped way are “existing”, classified by tag: trail = path / footway / cycleway / bridleway / steps; 4WD track = track; road = all other road classes. Consecutive intervals matching ways that share a name (or ref) are joined into one segment; brief interruptions under 50 m (road crossings, junction jitter) are absorbed. Adjacent unmatched runs separated by under 300 m of existing crossing are presented as one proposed segment (the crossing still counts toward its own class). Two local corrections from Gunnison Trails are applied: OSM way 334526647 (named “Van Tuyl Trail 431”, bicycle=no) is treated as a mis-named segment of Three Mile Creek Trail 434 with bicycle access, and one 0.2 mi out-and-back drawing artifact in the CB to Mill Creek TH section was excluded. Results are threshold-stable: matched share moves only 81.3% → 81.7% as tolerance varies 15 m → 40 m.

      Caveats. “No OSM match” is not proof of new construction — unmapped social or user-built tread shows up the same way. OSM tags are incomplete (about half the local track/path ways carry no surface tag), an OSM match does not establish legal bicycle access or public access, land ownership was not analyzed, and mileages are map-line miles. Verify flagged items against USFS/BLM travel-management data before submitting.
